Briefly summarize the DriverPass project. Who was the client? What type of system did they want you to design?
The DriverPass project was about designing a system for a driving education company that wanted to improve how students prepare for their driving tests. The client, DriverPass, needed an online system where students could take practice exams, schedule driving lessons, and track their progress. They also needed administrative features so staff could manage users, schedules, and content. My role was to take the information from the interview transcript and business requirements and turn it into a full system design using UML diagrams.

What did you do particularly well?
One thing I think I did well was translating the client’s ideas into structured diagrams and clear system requirements. I was able to identify the different users (students, instructors, and administrators) and show how they interact with the system. I also made sure to include security and access control, not just the basic features, which made the design feel more complete and realistic.

If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?
If I could revise one part of my work, I would improve the clarity and organization of my diagrams. While they represent the system correctly, I would refine them to make the flow even clearer and more detailed. I would also expand more on technical requirements like performance and scalability.

How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your system design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?
To interpret the user’s needs, I carefully reviewed the interview transcript and pulled out both direct requests and implied needs. For example, if students need to schedule lessons, the system must prevent double-booking and verify availability. Thinking about the user’s needs is important because a system should solve real problems, not just look good on paper. If you don’t focus on the user, the design can miss key functionality.

How do you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a system?
When I design software, I start by identifying stakeholders and breaking down functional and nonfunctional requirements. Then I use diagrams to visually map out how everything connects. In the future, I plan to continue using UML modeling and focus more on asking deeper questions early in the design process to anticipate issues before they happen. This project helped me better understand how to move from business requirements to a structured technical design
